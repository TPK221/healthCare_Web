Latest COVID-19 Information

As of 16 October 2022, a total of 4 867 192 confirmed cases and 36 417 deaths from COVID19 have been reported in Malaysia. The overall case fatality rate is 0.7%.

• In the past 14 days, Malaysia reported a decrease (6.6%) in new cases compared to the  previous two-week period.

• A total of 23 327 new cases were reported in the past 14 days. Of the newly reported cases,  99.7% were locally acquired.

• A total of 42 deaths were reported these past two weeks, a 33.3% decrease compared to the  previous two-week period. The case fatality rate for the past two weeks was 0.2%.

• The current COVID-19 intensive care unit (ICU) utilization rate is 9.8%, and the current COVID19 hospital bed utilization rate is 13.3%.

• In the past two weeks, 2 out of the 16 states/territories (Selangor and FT Putrajaya) reported  an increase in new COVID-19 cases compared to the previous two-week period.

• The highest 14-day incidence rates per 100 000 population were recorded in FT Putrajaya  (323.9), and FT Kuala Lumpur (286.8).

• In the past two weeks, 3 out of the 16 states/territories (Melaka, Pahang and Pulau Pinang)  reported an increase in COVID-19 deaths compared to the previous two-week period.

• As of 16 October 2022, 84.3% of Malaysia’s total population (98.2% of the adult population,  92.2% of the adolescent (12-17yrs) population and 43.3% of children (5-11yrs)) have received  their second dose of the COVID-19 vaccine. An estimated 49.7% of the total population has  received the third dose.

• In recent weeks, the number of new COVID-19 cases and COVID-19 deaths have been  decreasing. The ICU usage also remains relatively low.

• In the past 2 weeks, 100 whole genome sequencing (WGS) samples were submitted to  GISAID. The major circulating variant was BA.5 (82%). No XBB variant was identified.
Source : World Health Organization

Latest TYK Medical Centre Updates

Update on temporary changes to TYK Medical Centre urgent treatment centre

23rd November 2022

Temporary changes to the TYK Medical Centre urgent treatment centre (UTC) opening hours will remain in place until the New Year.  The UTC is open from 8am to 8.30pm, with patients being booked in until 7.30pm only.

To help better manage the demand for the UTC, and to ensure patients are receiving the right care in the right place, please contact your GP for dressing changes. The UTC is continuing to do first dressings and changing second, complex, dressings or those which have originated from the UTC.
